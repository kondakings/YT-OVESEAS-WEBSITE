STUDY IN USA

Why study in USA

The United States of America continues to be one of the most coveted places for higher education in the world and provides an invitation to students across the world. The USA Study is offering numerous world-class universities, sprawling with a wide array of multicultural environments and numerous latest research opportunities.This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about study in USA.

Intakes in the USA

The academic calendar in the USA provides three intakes in main:

Fall Intake(September) :

Most popular intake; it offers maximum courses and programs to students. Deadlines are usually December to March of the previous year.

Spring Intake (January) :

This is the time for admission for people missing the fall intake. Deadlines usually lie from July to October of the previous year.

Summer Intake (May) :

This is less frequent, especially in specific programs or courses. In most cases, deadlines run from January to March.

English Proficiency Requirements

Common tests accepted are:

IELTS

The minimum score required to study in USA is normally 6.5.

TOEFL

Typically, a minimum score of 90 would be required.

PTE

50–70 is usually through.

Tips to Enhance Chances of Admission

Solid Application

A holistic application backed by strong academic records, compelling essays, and excellent recommendation letters will make all the difference.

Early Preparation

Plan early so as to meet the deadlines and have all the documents required in place.

Do the research

On the various universities and what they look for. Some may have distinct criteria or even be quite flexible in the admission process.

Consult Experts

You may get in touch with educational counsellors or agencies who deal with overseas education for reaching out to help in the application process.

Conclusion

Knowing the intakes, targeting some of the top universities, and knowing requirements and alternatives in English proficiency will help you turn your dreams of studying in the USA into reality. The bottom line, whether one is to take the traditional route or one of the alternatives available to a student instead of taking the English proficiency tests, is good planning and presenting a nice application.
